1. Overview

Scale Insights is an Amazon advertising optimization platform that automates PPC work so you can focus on strategy. It connects to your Amazon Ads account, pulls performance data, and runs rule-based actions on a schedulethings like bid increases/decreases, budget pacing, search-term harvesting, and adding negatives.

Key Capabilities:

  • Rule-based bid management and budget optimization
  • Search term harvesting and negative keyword automation
  • Portfolio and campaign type scoping
  • Isolation strategies (SKC/Exec traffic funneling)
  • Change tracking and preview functionality
  • Dayparting, bulk edits, and performance alerts

  1. Flexibility

Scale Insights - Behind the Scenes:

  • We need to enable automation for our ASINs in Scale Insights
  • Scale Insights Rules work within ad groups, so a linked ASIN inside our target ad group is a requirement before adding any rules
  • Rules apply to all keywords and ASINs inside of our ad group

Important Implementation Points:

  1. We change the Target ACOS and clicks based on the Account/product
  2. We don't connect rules to Brand Defense campaigns (Brand KWs and PDP) since we want to have a manual approach on these
  3. We ONLY use Rules/Algorithms and NOT Strategic Objectives since they increase the complexity. Simple Scales, Fancy Fails

Key Limitations to Keep in Mind:

  • Precision Required: Scale Insights executes exactly as instructed. Any errors in your settings will be automated and applied across all campaigns, so it's crucial to be meticulous when configuring rules.
  • Steep Learning Curve: The platform can be challenging to master initially. However, once you understand the system, it becomes much easier to navigate and leverage effectively.

Why 30% NB ACOS Makes Sense:

  • Discovery Investment: Non-brand traffic requires higher ACOS tolerance during customer acquisition
  • Market Competition: Competitive categories often require 25-35% ACOS to maintain visibility
  • Attribution Gaps: Amazon attribution windows miss long-term brand building from discovery campaigns
  • Product Mix Reality: Most portfolios have products at different lifecycle stages requiring different targets

5. Implementation Methodology

Data Range Analysis

We aim to analyze data across different date ranges to read both the latest trends and the broader performance picture.

Our approach prioritizes shorter date ranges to make more immediate, data-driven decisions. If the data is not enough in shorter date range, then we analyze a longer period.

From 7 days, 14 days, 30 days, to 60 days.

Days to Exclude

Exclude:

  • The past 2 days due to Amazon Ads Attribution
  • Holidays like Prime Day, BFCM and the 2 days before and after each holiday

We don't want to have outlier dates in our data set since Scale Insights could increase/decrease the bids after analyzing these dates.

Days to Wait/Observe After Making a Change

If we take action every day, we'll be analyzing nearly the same data with only a one-day difference, which won't lead to meaningful changes.

Our approach is to take action, observe the results, and then act again based on the new data.

In our rules, we set a 7-day waiting period after making a bid change for each target.

  • If a keyword meets the set criteria, Scale Insights will adjust the bid and wait 7 days before re-evaluating that keyword
  • If the criteria aren't met, Scale Insights will continue analyzing the keyword daily until the conditions are satisfied

Rule Stacking and Priority

Stacking: We can "stack" rules on top of each other. This means that we can connect to one ad group different rules so Scale Insights will analyze multiple rules in order of priority.

Priority: Once we have multiple rules, Scale Insights needs to understand which rule they need to be analyzed first. The rule with the highest priority will be the first one to be analyzed, if the criteria are not met, then Scale Insights will analyze the rule with the second highest priority and so on.

6. ASP-Based Gates

Different Average Selling Price (ASP) ranges require different data gates and thresholds for optimization decisions.

< $20 15 clicks or $8 spend 20 clicks & 1.7 CPA 25 clicks & 2 CPA
$20-50 20 clicks or $12 spend 25 clicks & 1.5 CPA 35 clicks & 2 CPA
$50-120 25 clicks or $20 spend 35 clicks & 1.4 CPA 45 clicks & 1.8 CPA
> $120 30 clicks or $30 spend 40 clicks & 1.3 CPA 55 clicks & 1.6 CPA

CPA Calculation: If no CPA target is stored, compute CPA proxy = Price × Target ACOS

General Guideline: Higher-priced products require more clicks for statistical significance due to lower predicted conversion rates. Adjust thresholds based on your specific product performance data.

7. Lifecycle Guardrails

Launch (0-14d)

  • ✓ Bid moves ≤ 5-8%/day
  • ✓ Bid floor at 70% of Suggested Low
  • ✓ No negatives until 30 clicks & 2 target CPA

Scale (15-60d)

  • ✓ Bid moves 8-12%
  • ✓ Allow Neg Exact at 20 clicks & 1.5 CPA
  • ✓ Standard ASP gates apply

Stabilize (60d+)

  • ✓ Tighter floors/ceilings
  • ✓ Allow pause rules
  • ✓ Neg Phrase for bad themes

8. Rule Types & Setup

Bidding Rules

Goal: Bid optimization is a crucial element in effectively optimizing and scaling our ad account. We will leverage Scale Insights' automated rules to achieve this.

  • Flexibility in customization
  • Full control over bid adjustments
  • Transparency in rule execution
  • Precision required in setup

Negative Rules

The end-goal is automating the process of adding negative rules only to discovery campaigns and auto campaigns.

We only add Negative Targeting rules to Discovery and Auto campaigns since the idea behind these campaigns is to capture new traffic, and therefore some of the Search Terms would be irrelevant to our business.

Key Setup Summary:

  • How many clicks? Based on Conversion Rate and Tolerance of Error
  • Data range to analyze? 7, 14, 30 and 60 days - in this order
  • Days to Exclude? Last 2 and holidays (+ 2 days before and after)
  • Days to Observe? 7 Days

9. Copy-Paste Ready Filters

General Exclusions:

Portfolio name does NOT contain: DEF, BRAND, BKWS, PROTECT Campaign name does NOT contain: DEF, BRAND, BKWS, PROTECT Status = Enabled

Negative Rules Additional:

Campaign name does NOT contain: EX, SKC, -EX Ad group name does NOT contain: EXEC, SKC

Why These Exclusions?

  • General Exclusions: Protect brand/defense campaigns from non-brand optimization logic
  • Negative Rules Additional: Prevent adding negatives to winner campaigns (EX/SKC) that are already profitable
  • Status = Enabled: Only applies rules to active campaigns/ad groups

10. Change Log Verification

At-a-Glance Checks (Fast Pass)

  • Run time & rule name match what you scheduled
  • Action count ≈ preview count (within 10%)
  • Scope sanity: only the intended portfolios/campaign types touched (NB only; no BR/DEF)
  • Entity sanity: right layer changed (e.g., keyword bids, not ad-group bids, for that rule)
  • No extremes: no moves beyond your floors/ceilings or odd 0→huge jumps

Fields to Read in Each Log Line

Common to All Actions
  • ✓ Timestamp & Account/Marketplace
  • ✓ Rule Name / Version
  • ✓ Entity Path: Portfolio → Campaign → Ad Group → (Keyword/Target)
  • ✓ Action: increase/decrease/set/pause/add negative/add keyword
  • ✓ Before → After values (+ % change)
  • ✓ Status: succeeded / skipped (with reason)
Bidding-Specific
  • ✓ Previous Bid → New Bid and % change
  • ✓ Safeguards hit: bid floor/ceiling
  • ✓ Reason snapshot: ACOS vs target, clicks/spend thresholds

Cross-Checks (3-5 minutes)

  • Counts: preview vs log action count (±10%)
  • Spot-check 5 items: ensure none are BR/DEF or EX/SKC (for negatives)
  • Guardrails: confirm no entries breached floors/ceilings
  • Skips: skim "skipped" reasons—common culprits are dayparting, already paused, or below floor
  • Results trend (next day): no sudden CPC spikes on winners; no volume drop on brand
Red Flags
  • Negatives added to brand/defense or to Exec/SKC campaigns
  • Bids pushed past floor/ceiling or >15% move in a single run
  • Large mismatch between preview and actual actions
  • Many "skipped" due to mis-scoped filters
  • Oscillation (up then down daily on the same keyword)

📊 Specific Change Log Analysis Procedures:

Bidding Change Analysis:
  • Dashboard → Change Logs → Bidding
  • Sort ACOS from highest to lowest
  • Verify changes match criteria
  • Sort by Adjustment (highest to lowest)
  • Check if significant changes make sense
Negative Rule Verification:
  • Dashboard → Change Logs → Negative Rule
  • Set date range to last 30/60 days
  • Review negated keywords/ASINs via dropdown
  • Reverse unwanted negations via Negative Keywords

11. Defense Playbook

BKWS (Branded Keywords Search)

Campaigns bidding on your own brand terms + close variants/misspellings.

Goal: Block competitors on your name and keep message/TOS placement
Approach: Use CPC ceilings; maintain modest TOS multiplier; focus on coverage, not expansion
Negatives: Only block obvious junk intent (customer service, refund, manual)

Def PDP (Defensive Product Detail Page)

SP campaigns targeting your own ASINs so competitor ads don't steal shoppers.

Goal: Cross-sell/upsell within your brand; prevent competitor takeover
Approach: Prioritize hero ASINs; keep budgets modest but always-on
Negatives: Add negative ASINs/categories for serial bleeders with 0 orders

Why Exclude Defense from Normal Rules?

  • Different Intent: Branded traffic converts high and is not "new" demand
  • Avoid Overspend: Low ACOS on brand can trick automation into over-bidding
  • Protect Coverage: Negative/harvesting rules can accidentally block brand terms
  • Cleaner Measurement: Keeping defense separate makes TACOS/"incremental" analysis honest
  • Stability: Brand & PDP defense should be "steady coverage," not whipsawed by discovery rules

Brand CPC Caps by Market:

US: $0.70
CA: C$0.90
UK: £0.55
DE: €0.60

Lift +20% only during promos, then revert. Use rule: New Bid = min(Suggested Mid, CPC Cap)

14. Weekly Automation Audit Checklist

📅 Frequency: Once a Week

🎯 Objectives:

  • Ensure implemented rules are correct
  • Verify rule criteria accuracy
  • Confirm bidding and negative rules apply to all relevant campaigns

✅ 1. Link Ads with ASINs Check

📍 Navigation:

Dashboard → Ads Insights → Link Ads with ASINs

🔍 Action Steps:

  1. Check if all ad groups are linked to active and automated ASINs
  2. Link unlinked campaigns to applicable, top-spending Child ASIN
  3. Quick RED highlight check: Dashboard → Automation → Bidding rule
  4. If RED highlight appears, automate the ASIN or link to automated Child ASIN

✅ 2. Inactive ASIN Management

📍 Navigation:

Dashboard → Ads Insights → Link Ads with ASINs → Linked Ads tab

🔍 Action Steps:

  1. Filter Status by "Inactive"
  2. Ensure no active campaigns linked to inactive ASINs
  3. Unlink active campaigns from inactive ASINs
  4. Relink to active Child ASINs
  5. Ensure ASINs are not automated

✅ 3. Top Spending Campaign Rules Check

📍 Navigation:

Dashboard → Automation → Bidding Rule

🔍 Action Steps:

  1. Sort campaigns from Highest to Lowest by Spend
  2. Ensure all top spending campaigns in SP/SB/SD have rules applied
  3. Verify rules still comply with account's goals
  4. Double-check criteria of rules, especially Maintain rule

✅ 4. New Campaigns Rules Check

📍 Navigation:

Dashboard → Automation → Bidding Rule

🔍 Action Steps:

  1. Sort Start Date column from most recent to least recent
  2. Check SP/SB/SD campaigns for applied rules
  3. Apply rules to newest campaigns if missing
  4. Verify linked ASIN is automated and correct

✅ 5. Automation Change Logs Review

📍 Navigation:

Dashboard → Automation → Change Logs → Bidding

🔍 Action Steps:

  1. Sort ACOS from highest to lowest
  2. Verify changes align with criteria
  3. Sort by Adjustment (highest to lowest)
  4. Check if significant changes make sense
  5. Note: Maintain rule changes don't appear in logs

✅ 6. Automation Negatives Check

📍 Navigation:

Dashboard → Automation → Negative Rule

🔍 Action Steps:

  1. Sort campaigns from most recent to oldest
  2. Apply Negative rule only on Discovery campaigns
  3. Exclude Exact and Phrase campaigns
  4. Check Change Logs → Negative Rule tab (last 30/60 days)
  5. Reverse unwanted negations via Negative Keywords

📊 Weekly Audit Completion Criteria

  • All active campaigns linked to automated ASINs (no RED highlights)
  • Top 20 spending campaigns have appropriate rules applied
  • New campaigns (last 7 days) have rules applied within 24 hours
  • Change logs reviewed and anomalies investigated
  • Negative rule working correctly on Discovery campaigns only
  • No unwanted negatives added to Exact/SKC campaigns

16. Troubleshooting Guide

🚨 Critical Issues

Problem: Rules Not Executing

Symptoms: No changes in Change Logs, campaigns not being optimized

Solutions:
  1. Check if campaigns are linked to automated ASINs
  2. Verify rule filters aren't too restrictive
  3. Ensure campaigns meet minimum data thresholds
  4. Check if dayparting is blocking execution
  5. Verify rule schedule is active and correctly configured

Problem: RED Highlight in ASIN Column

Symptoms: Red highlighting indicates unautomated ASINs

Solutions:
  1. Go to Link Ads with ASINs
  2. Find unlinked ad groups and link to appropriate Child ASIN
  3. Ensure the target ASIN is set to "Automated" status
  4. Wait 24 hours for system to update

⚠️ Performance Issues

Problem: Unexpected Bid Changes

Symptoms: Bids moving outside expected ranges

Solutions:
  1. Check rule criteria and thresholds
  2. Review bid floors and ceilings
  3. Verify data range settings aren't including outlier dates
  4. Check if holiday exclusions are properly configured

Problem: Wrong Campaigns Being Targeted

Symptoms: Brand/Defense campaigns affected by rules

Solutions:
  1. Review portfolio/campaign name filters
  2. Add exclusions: DEF, BRAND, BKWS, PROTECT
  3. Check campaign naming conventions
  4. Update rule scope to exclude problematic portfolios

❓ Common Questions

Q: How long before changes take effect?

A: Changes typically appear in Amazon within 2-4 hours of execution. Check Change Logs to confirm successful application. Allow 24-48 hours to see performance impact.

Q: Why don't I see Maintain rule changes in logs?

A: Maintain rules are designed to keep bids stable when performance is within target. They don't make changes, so no log entries appear. This is normal behavior.

Q: Can I reverse negative keyword additions?

A: Yes. Go to Negative Keywords, find the term, select it, and click Remove. Changes take effect within 2-4 hours.

Q: How to check weekly/monthly performance trends?

A: Use Weekly Trends and Monthly Trends for comprehensive performance analysis including Sales, ACOS, ROI, and conversion metrics.

📞 When to Contact Support

  • Rules stop executing entirely for 24+ hours
  • Data synchronization issues with Amazon Ads account
  • Unexpected large-scale bid changes affecting budget
  • ASIN linking issues that persist after troubleshooting
  • Platform errors or system-wide performance issues
